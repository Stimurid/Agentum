# WAVE_04D_MEMORY_TRUTH_REPEAT

This wave joined memory, truth-check, and repetition routing into one operating picture.

Key takeaways:

- recovered does not mean verified;
- repetition can be deepening, looping, stale instruction, or recovery need;
- truth-check is a small anti-accommodation intervention, not a moral sermon.

What this wave contributed:

- epistemic status vocabulary;
- repeat-router concept;
- memory-authority risk framing used in the v0 prompt guard.

Operational consequence:

The more familiar a remembered pattern feels, the more carefully its authority must be checked.
